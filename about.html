About Us

What drives us

We believe the most compelling science begins at an interface — where a solid meets a fluid, where geometry meets physics, where a simple structure gives rise to complex function.

At FSRL, we do not start with applications. We start with questions. Why does a porous shell deform the way it does when fluid passes through? How can a hydrogel's swelling encode a sequence of motions without any external control? What happens when structure itself becomes the mechanism?

These questions lead us to the intersection of fluid mechanics, soft matter physics, and structural design. Our work is grounded in mechanics — the language of forces, deformations, and instabilities — but our curiosity extends wherever the physics takes us.

How we work

Research in our lab is built on two rhythms.

The first is a weekly one-on-one conversation between each student and the PI. These are not status reports — they are thinking sessions. An hour to slow down, question assumptions, and reshape ideas together. We believe that the best research insights emerge not from presentations, but from unhurried dialogue.

The second is a weekly group exchange where every member shares their current work with the rest of the lab. This is where unexpected connections happen — where someone working on hydrogel swelling finds resonance with someone studying porous shell mechanics. We keep these sessions informal and curiosity-driven.

Beyond these structures, we value a culture of intellectual honesty. We ask hard questions because we care about getting the science right, and we support each other through the inevitable uncertainties of research.
